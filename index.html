What keeps me occupied?

I am completing my final year of BE Mechatronics at the University of Technology Sydney (UTS) while concurrently working as an Undergraduate Engineer at Space Machines Company. Primarily my work revolves around evaluating electronic solutions and developing documentation and integration prototypes using PCB design and firmware development. University extra-curricular activities include advisory positions within UTS Robotics Society.

How did I get here?

Throughout my career, I have had hands-on experience working on projects involving microcontrollers, sensors, actuators, ROS and computer aided design. I really got to explore these areas during my 3 years as a Research Assistant at UTS Robotics Institute. In addition, I was an active member of UTS Robotics Society with experiences with participating and organising competitions, community outreach, colaborative events, workshops and holding a range of executive positions including President. One of the most rewarding experiences I have had, was being invited by UTS to teach a robotics studio course to a wonderful group of students.

Personal passion projects

I've never been one to throw my feet up after the 9-5. I find myself constantly exploring and learning about the wonderful world of FOSS which has manifested in daily driving Linux, writing scripts, guides and self-hosting storage, networking and container systems. When I'm not falling down that rabbit hole, I enjoy the challenges of leading outdoor adventure groups on a mix of hiking, biking, 4wding and camping trips.
